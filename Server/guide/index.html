McRubes Media Server

Your personal Netflix — quick guide to watching and requesting content

🎬 Watching Movies & TV Shows

1
Open Plex on your TV, phone, or browser On a browser, go to mcrubes.local:32401/web or use the Plex app on any device.
2
Sign in with your Plex account If you don't have one, create a free account at plex.tv — it takes 2 minutes.
3
Find McRubes in your server list Look for "McRubes" under "More > Your Servers" and you'll see Movies and TV Shows.
4
Pick something and press play That's it. Subtitles, resume, and watchlists all work like Netflix.
Tip: If a video won't play, try pressing the settings icon (⚙) during playback and switching quality to "Original" or a lower option.

📥 Requesting New Movies or TV Shows

Don't see something you want to watch? Request it — it usually shows up within an hour.

1
Go to Overseerr mcrubes.local:5055 — sign in with your Plex account (same one from above).
2
Search for what you want Type the movie or show name in the search bar at the top.
3
Click Request Hit the orange "Request" button. For TV shows you can request specific seasons.
4
Wait a bit, then watch It downloads automatically and appears in Plex when ready — usually within an hour.
Note: Watched movies and shows are automatically cleaned up after 14 days to save space. Request again anytime if you want to rewatch.

📚 Books & Kindle

The server has a book library too. You can read books in your browser or send them to your Kindle.

1
Open Calibre Web Go to mcrubes.local:8084. Login: mcrubes / mcrubes
2
Find a book and click Send to Kindle The envelope icon next to any book sends it directly to your Kindle.
Want a book that isn't in the library? Let Mike know and he can add it.

🛠 Something Not Working?

Problem Try this
Plex won't load Wait 1–2 minutes and refresh. The server may be restarting.
Video buffers or won't play Tap the ⚙ icon during playback → lower the quality or select "Original".
Can't find a show in Plex It may not be downloaded yet. Request it in Overseerr and wait an hour.
Request says "already requested" Someone already requested it — it'll show up soon.
Pages won't load at all Make sure you're on the home Wi-Fi. These links only work at home.
